Our mission

Sesnsational Cities was founded on the principal that life is all about learning and exploration. That our learning journey never ends and the best way to grow is through experiencing something new. Also, nothing contributes more to peace in our world than increasing our understanding about other people. Cities are the window into the hearts, minds and souls of a nation s and by exploring them we can learn about the world around us and ourselves.

"Not all those who wander are lost "

- J.R.R. Tolkien

The mission of this website is to introduce fascinating cities from across the globe. To begin, we will select some of the better know cities and then move to some of the hidden jewels contained in our fascinating world. Each page will contain a little about the background of the city and then a few of that cities notable features. This is not intended as a travel site, but rather as a way to spark understanding and interest in the world around us and hopefully help to develop a travel bug within you. So each page will just contain brief snippets of information that will hopefully increase a spark in you to go and search for additional information.

"We travel, some of us forever, to seek other places, other lives, other souls."

- Anais Nin

To this end we need your help. We would like you to help drive the content of our site. So please take the time to share with us what cities you have been to that you found fascinating. Or what cities would ypu like to find out more about. We will be constantly checking your responses and adding new cities. We look forward to hearing from you. Until then we hope you enjoy your journey.

Kyoto, Japan

Kansai is made up of four major cities. There is the bustling neon jugle of Osaka, Japan's second largest city. Towards the west is the beautiful city of Kobe which is located on a narrow peice of land that is sandwiched by a mountain and the sea. To the east are the two ancient capitals of Nara and Kyoto. Nara was the capital of Japan from 710 to 794. But the jewel in Japan's amazing site-seeing crown is the city of Kyoto. The last time it was the capital of Japan was in 1869.

"Even in Kyoto

Hearing the cuckoo's cry

I long for Kyoto"

- Matsuo Basho

There are literally thousands of Buddhist temples and Shinto shrines in Kyoto. One of the great joys is to wander along the streets and discover some ancient small temple nestled between a couple of modern buildings. Some of Japan's key sites are ontained with this city, that is itself ringed by mountains.The variety, historical span and sheer number of World Heritage Sites in Kyoto is unrivaled in Japan. Nijo-jo Castle and the sixteen temples and shrines were selected as World Heritage Sites by UNESCO in 1994. Kyoto is a wonderful example of a modern, working Japanese city that is sprinkled with sites that contain it's ancient past. Please take the time to read more about or visit and explore this wonderful city.

Top 3 sites in Kyoto
Kinkakuji "Kin" means gold, so it is the golden temple. It is one of the most popular tourist sites in Japan. The temple's character changes with the season. You can find out more about it here: Link to Wikipedia.
Kiyomizu It is a Buddhist temple located in Eastern Kyoto. The current structure was built in 1633. The views of Kyoto are breathtaking. You can find out more information about it here: Link to Wikipedia.
Nijo This castle was completed in 1626. It is made up of two rings of fortification. The inside of the castle contains beautiful traditional rooms. You can find out more information about it here: Link to Wikipedia.
